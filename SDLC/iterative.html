Iterative Model

The Iterative Model is a software development methodology that involves breaking down a project into smaller, manageable iterations or cycles. Each iteration encompasses planning, design, implementation, testing, and evaluation.

Advantages

  • 1. Allows for flexibility and adaptability to changing requirements.

  • 2. Early and continuous delivery of valuable software increments.

  • 3. Enhanced collaboration between stakeholders throughout the development process.

  • 4. Improved risk management through iterative feedback and mitigation.

  • 5. Facilitates continuous improvement and refinement of the software product.

Disadvantages

  • 1. Requires a high level of customer involvement and feedback.

  • 2. Can result in scope creep if not managed effectively.

  • 3. Requires careful planning and coordination of iterative cycles.

  • 4. May lead to increased development time and cost.

  • 5. Dependence on skilled development and testing teams.
