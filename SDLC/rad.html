RAD (Rapid Application Development)

Rapid Application Development (RAD) is a software development methodology that prioritizes rapid prototyping and iterative development to accelerate the delivery of high-quality software applications. It emphasizes user feedback, collaboration, and minimal planning upfront.

Advantages

  • 1. Rapid prototyping leads to quick feedback and iteration.

  • 2. Enhanced user involvement and satisfaction.

  • 3. Reduced development time and cost.

  • 4. Flexible and adaptable to changing requirements.

  • 5. Early identification and mitigation of risks.

Disadvantages

  • 1. Can lead to scope creep if not managed effectively.

  • 2. Requires highly skilled developers and designers.

  • 3. May not be suitable for large-scale projects with complex requirements.

  • 4. Dependency on customer availability and involvement.

  • 5. Less emphasis on documentation and formal processes.
