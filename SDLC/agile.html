Agile

Agile is a Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) model that emphasizes iterative development, incremental delivery, and flexibility in responding to changing requirements.
It promotes adaptive planning, evolutionary development, early delivery, and continuous improvement.

Advantages

  • 1. Flexibility: Agile allows for flexibility in responding to changing requirements, ensuring the final product meets evolving needs.

  • 2. Customer Collaboration: Agile promotes customer collaboration throughout the development process, ensuring the end product meets customer expectations.

  • 3. Rapid Delivery: Agile emphasizes incremental delivery, enabling rapid release of usable and valuable software increments.

  • 4. Transparency: Agile practices such as daily stand-ups and regular demos ensure transparency and visibility into project progress.

  • 5. Continuous Improvement: Agile encourages continuous improvement through regular retrospectives, allowing teams to reflect on their processes and make adjustments.

Disadvantages

  • 1. Lack of Predictability: Agile's adaptive nature may lead to unpredictability in project timelines and outcomes.

  • 2. Resource Intensive: Agile requires a high level of collaboration and communication, which can be resource-intensive for distributed teams.

  • 3. Resistance to Change: Agile requires a cultural shift and may face resistance from stakeholders accustomed to traditional development methodologies.

  • 4. Scope Creep: Agile's focus on flexibility may result in scope creep if not managed effectively, leading to increased project complexity.

  • 5. Dependency on Team Collaboration: Agile's success relies heavily on effective collaboration and communication within cross-functional teams.
