Incremental

    The Incremental model is a software development methodology that divides the development process into smaller, manageable increments or stages. Each increment builds upon the previous one, gradually adding functionality to the software product.

    Advantages

    • 1. Allows for early delivery of working software.

    • 2. Reduced risk of project failure.

    • 3. Enhanced flexibility to accommodate changing requirements.

    • 4. Improved customer satisfaction through incremental releases.

    • 5. Provides opportunities for feedback and adjustments throughout the development process.

    Disadvantages

    • 1. Requires careful planning and coordination of increments.

    • 2. Increased complexity due to multiple iterations.

    • 3. Potential for integration issues between increments.

    • 4. Dependency on skilled development and testing teams.

    • 5. May lead to higher overall cost compared to other models.
